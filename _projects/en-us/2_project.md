<h3 style="font-weight: normal;">Project Goal:</h3>
<ul>
    <li>To develop, create, and assemble a rehabilitation intelligent robotic exoskeleton capable of assisting in the restoration of lost physical functions, conducting longer and more intensive therapy sessions, freeing therapeutic staff from manually supporting the patient, determining the required load for the patient, monitoring, guiding, and recording the recovery process over various periods, and using patient actions in combination with computer games to maintain patient motivation during lengthy weeks and months of therapy programs.</li>
    <li>The intelligent robotic exoskeleton should be bidirectional, including various input and feedback methods (force, motion, biosignals).</li>
    <li>The intelligent robotic exoskeleton should be applicable to the whole body as well as to any part of the body (modular – disassemblable without losing functions applicable to any part of the body).</li>
    <li>The rehabilitation intelligent robotic exoskeleton should allow for the restoration of human mobility functions that surpass those provided by a wheelchair, which is a common mobility aid.</li>
</ul>

<h3 style="font-weight: normal;">Project Objectives:</h3>
<ul>
    <li>Development, design, and assembly of a prototype rehabilitation intelligent robotic exoskeleton for restoring <b>upper limb functions</b>, conducting research, tests, and formulating conclusions.</li>
    <li>Development, design, and assembly of a prototype rehabilitation intelligent robotic exoskeleton for restoring <b>lower limb functions</b>, conducting research, tests, and formulating conclusions.</li>
    <li>Development, design, and assembly of a prototype rehabilitation intelligent robotic exoskeleton for restoring <b>spine functions</b>, conducting research, tests, and formulating conclusions.</li>
    <li>Development, design, and assembly of a prototype rehabilitation intelligent robotic exoskeleton for restoring <b>whole body functions</b>, conducting research, tests, and formulating conclusions.</li>
    <li>Development of an <b>intelligent platform</b> for controlling the rehabilitation intelligent robotic exoskeleton.</li>
    <li>Development, design, and assembly of an industrial <b>sample of the rehabilitation intelligent robotic exoskeleton.</b></li>
</ul>

<h3 style="font-weight: normal;">Project Hypothesis:</h3>
<p>All lost human functions can be restored.</p>

<h3 style="font-weight: normal;">Relevance:</h3>
<p>The intelligent robotic exoskeleton will change the lives of people with special needs, restore their lost functions, and give them the ability to perform ordinary tasks such as climbing stairs independently, eating, and fully communicating with others daily. Additionally, the use of exoskeletons will help create an inclusive, open environment for people with special needs.</p>
